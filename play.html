The Learned Women

ACT IV - SCENE VI.--ARMANDE, CLITANDRE.
ARM.

I am very sorry to see, Sir, that things are not going quite according to your views.

CLI.

I shall go and do all I can not to leave this serious anxiety upon your mind.

ARM.

I am afraid that your efforts will not be very successful.

CLI.

You may perhaps see that your fears are without foundation.

ARM.

I hope it may be so.

CLI.

I am persuaded that I shall have all your help.

ARM.

Yes, I will second you with all my power.

CLI.

And I shall be sure to be most grateful.
